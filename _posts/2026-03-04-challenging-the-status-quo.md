My family and I all have one thing in common: no matter where we move or what we do, we will never be able to keep a plant alive. Due to this, it is a common joke around my Irish side of the family that we were safe from the potato famine because we were never able to grow them in the first place. 

Although there have been many generations of repeated agricultural failure throughout my lineage, I have recently decided to challenge the status quo and grow a **Venus Flytrap**. 

### The Arrival
I received the plant on February 14th, 2026, and was immediately perplexed. I originally picked this plant because I assumed it was low maintenance—I was proved wrong within my first day. 

> **The Requirements:**
> * **Soil:** Specific nutrient-poor carnivorous mix 
> * **Light:** Intensive daily sunlight
> * **Hydration:** Strictly distilled water or rainwater
> * **Environment:** A specialized bog-friendly pot



### The Cost of Determination
The cost of the plant quadrupled just to ensure it would survive the first week. After seeing the total purchase cost, I was very close to throwing in the towel, but then I remembered all of the jokes, and my determination was restored. 

I luckily found a friend who had experience with Venus flytraps, and together we built a grow station that satisfied all of the plant’s needs. I have yet to feed it a live bug, but that will be the next step, and I will include a video in the next blog update!
